Step Up! American Association for Rwandan Women

This website for Step Up! was my first attempt at building a site from scratch. Step Up! is a non-profit that works with victims of sexual violence and survivors of the 1994 genocides in Rwanda. As a member of their advisory board, I felt it necessary that the original website be updated to include more information about the organization's work and to be more accessible and attractive for all users.

To build this site I used Bootstraps, HTML5, CSS, PHP, and Photoshop to create all the infographics shown. I am continuing to work with other board members to improve the website to ensure it is suitable and meets the needs of the organization. I hope to have it completely finished within the next couple months so that it can ultimately replace Step Up!'s outdated website.

Check out my work @ Step Up! American Association for Rwandan Women

LaunchCode

LaunchCode is where I developed my interest in front-end web development and got my taste of real world code. While working as a communications intern, I was asked to edit and create additional pages after LaunchCode's original and very talented designer and front-end developer had left the organization to attend graduate school. I created two additional pages within their Ruby on Rails framework to match the previous design and aesthetics of the website while also trying to add minor variations into the layout. I also made global website changes to the font-size and landing photos. This small but very welcome and eye-opening opportunity taught me a tremendous amount about Git and internal file arrangements for large websites such as LaunchCode's. It was also my first introduction to Bootstraps and Javascript.
